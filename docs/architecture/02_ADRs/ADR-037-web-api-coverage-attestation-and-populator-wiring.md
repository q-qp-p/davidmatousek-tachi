### D-1: Combined-vs-Split ADR Scope (RESOLVED single combined per Q-PM-1)

**Decision**: Single combined ADR-037 documenting all four work streams (F-8 attestation + F-A3 wiring + record-shape extension + aggregator filter). The Architect MEDIUM-A recommendation to spin off the taxonomy YAML record-shape extension into a separate ADR-027.x increment was declined; the extension is documented inline at D-7 within this ADR.

**Stream**: Cross-cutting

**Context**: The F-241 plan-day surfaced Q-PM-1 as the single most consequential structural question of the feature: should F-241 be documented in one ADR (combining F-8 attestation, F-A3 populator wiring, record-shape extension, and aggregator filter) or in two coordinated ADRs (one for the F-8/Stream 4 attestation surface, one for the F-A3/Stream 1 populator wiring + ADR-027 D7 record-shape extension)? The cumulative F-A3 deferral lineage (ADR-032 / ADR-034 D-8 / ADR-035 D-10 / ADR-036 D-10 — four-feature cumulative debt) closes at this ADR via D-10; fragmenting that closure across two ADRs would complicate the citation chain for future maintainers tracing the deferral history.

**Consequence**: One artifact carries the full F-241 architectural narrative across all four streams; D-numbered structure makes the streams independently readable for stream-focused review. The asymmetry with F-1 / F-2 / F-4 (each shipping a single-stream ADR) is acceptable because F-241 is intrinsically multi-stream by design — it is the closure feature, not a Tier 1/2 net-new or enrichment feature. The ADR-027 forward-pointer addendum (M-1 carry-forward) is added at T060 (Wave 6.1) cross-linking ADR-037 D-7 from the ADR-027 footer ("Extended in ADR-037 D-7 — see also"), preserving the ADR-027 readability for adopters arriving at the taxonomy schema from upstream context.

### D-2: F-A3 11-Host Expansion (Architect HIGH-A — includes `prompt-injection` + `agent-autonomy`)

**Decision**: Wire `source_attribution` populators across all 11 candidate host agents — including `prompt-injection` and `agent-autonomy` per Architect HIGH-A — because the aggregator reads only `source_attribution[].id` directly without distinguishing agent provenance. The 11 hosts are: 5 STRIDE-heavy (`spoofing`, `tampering`, `info-disclosure`, `privilege-escalation`, `repudiation`), 2 STRIDE-extended (`denial-of-service`, `tool-abuse`), 2 ML-tier (`data-poisoning`, `model-theft`), and 2 LLM-tier (`prompt-injection`, `agent-autonomy`). Combined with 3 pre-existing F-1/F-2/F-4 net-new agents that already populate `source_attribution`, F-241 brings detection-tier coverage to 14/14 files (FR-005 BLOCKER).

**Stream**: Stream 1

**Context**: At PRD time, the question of whether `prompt-injection` and `agent-autonomy` belonged in F-A3 scope was open — they have direct LLM Top 10 / Agentic Security Top 10 coverage but emit findings whose source_attribution had been considered "implicit by prefix" (LLM-* findings cite OWASP LLM06 by default; AG-* findings cite OWASP ASI04 by default). Architect HIGH-A established at PRD review time that the aggregator at `scripts/extract-report-data.py:1166-1170` reads `finding.source_attribution[].id` directly with NO implicit prefix-attribution heuristic — meaning that without explicit populator wiring on `prompt-injection` and `agent-autonomy`, those agents' findings would emit empty `source_attribution: []` arrays at runtime and contribute zero to the OWASP LLM01 / ASI01 / ASI06 / ASI08 / ASI10 coverage_percentage. The closure-verification-wave-2 audit at `_internal/strategy/BLP-01-threat-coverage.md` confirmed the gap empirically — pre-F-241, every baseline rendered LLM01 and ASI01 as Gap on the §6 Coverage Matrix even though `prompt-injection` and `agent-autonomy` agents were cited extensively in detection workflows. Wave 2 absorbed +1 day for the HIGH-A 11-host expansion; the LLM-tier agents were sized at smaller wiring effort than the STRIDE-heavy hosts (smaller existing pattern-category surfaces, narrower trigger-keyword fan-out).

**Consequence**: The 11-host F-A3 scope is the largest single-feature populator-wiring fan-out in BLP-01 history — 7× the F-3 single-host enrichment surface, 5.5× the F-5 two-host surface, 3.7× the F-6 three-host surface, 2.2× the F-7 five-host surface. Wave 1 (Days 1–5) covered 5 STRIDE-heavy hosts with pair-authoring (senior-backend-engineer + security-analyst) per Team-Lead MEDIUM-R1 to keep daily load within 80% cap; Wave 2 (Days 6–11) absorbed `prompt-injection` and `agent-autonomy` plus the remaining four ML/STRIDE-extended hosts. Post-merge `grep -l "source_attribution" .claude/agents/tachi/*.md | wc -l` returns **14** (verified at Wave 5.1 T051 pre-edit; preserved through Wave 5.2). Test infrastructure at `tests/scripts/test_backward_compatibility.py::DETECTION_AGENT_PATHS` shifts from 4 entries (pre-F-241) to 2 entries (post-F-241) — `prompt-injection.md` and `agent-autonomy.md` are removed from the F-142 zero-edit invariant and added to `DETECTION_PATTERN_REF_ENRICHMENT_HOSTS` frozenset; the F-1 + F-2 net-new agents (`output-integrity.md` + `misinformation.md`) remain the only entries left in the F-142 zero-edit invariant on this branch.

### D-5: Tactical-Grouping Out-of-Scope Strategy on ATT&CK Enterprise (TA0005/7/8/9/10/11/40 + curator extension TA0112/TA0043)

**Decision**: Apply tactical-grouping `out_of_scope: true` rationale to 7 ATT&CK Enterprise tactics that are runtime/IR-only (not design-time-relevant): TA0005 Defense Evasion, TA0007 Discovery, TA0008 Lateral Movement, TA0009 Collection, TA0010 Exfiltration, TA0011 Command and Control, TA0040 Impact. In-scope tactics (TA0001 Initial Access, TA0002 Execution, TA0003 Persistence, TA0004 Privilege Escalation, TA0006 Credential Access, TA0042 Resource Development) receive per-item `out_of_scope` rationale on individual runtime-only sub-techniques (T042 scope, conservatively limited to T1078.004 Cloud Accounts per data-model.md §2 line 110 explicit example). A curator extension at T041 build time added TA0112 (Defense Impairment) + TA0043 (Reconnaissance) as 2 derived-rationale tactics not enumerated in data-model.md §5 — the spec authority recommendation is to update data-model.md §5 in a non-breaking follow-on commit (+2 entries enumerating TA0112 + TA0043) before delivery; if not absorbed at follow-on commit, the curator extension is documented at this D-5 narrative as a build-time discovery and the spec authority gap is a known-deferred item.

**Stream**: Stream 3

**Context**: The OWASP MITRE ATT&CK Enterprise framework carries ~700+ active sub-techniques across 14 tactics (post-spec STIX 2.1 expansion at T041 retrieval). Tachi reasons over architecture diagrams at design time; it does NOT observe runtime process behavior, file-system manipulation, in-memory injection, network telemetry, EDR session monitoring, or DLP. The seven tactics enumerated above describe attacker behavior whose detection inherently requires runtime observation surfaces (active-malware code paths, post-compromise pivoting, active reconnaissance queries, active data harvesting, active data egress, active C2 channels, active impact actions). Without an Out-of-Scope filter, the coverage_percentage denominator would inflate to ~600+ across these tactics' member items — most of which tachi has no design-time analog for — producing artificially-low coverage percentages (~5–10%) that misrepresent the actual design-time threat coverage that tachi delivers. The tactical-grouping approach (Stream 3 T040 audit catalog) applies one verbatim rationale string per tactic to all member items uniformly — preserving F-A2 referential-integrity (Out-of-Scope items remain catalog-resolvable) while steering the items out of the aggregator denominator (Stream 4 D-8 filter).

The tactic-level rationale catalog is sourced from `data-model.md` §5 (lines 192–198) and was preserved character-for-character verbatim by T041 across all member items of the 7 enumerated OOS tactics. T041's STIX 2.1 expansion to 701 records surfaced two post-spec MITRE-published tactics (TA0112 Defense Impairment + TA0043 Reconnaissance) not enumerated in data-model.md §5; per FR-024 primary-source-correction discipline, the curator chose to honor MITRE's current taxonomy and added 2 derived rationale strings following T040's narrative pattern (`"Tactic TA0112 operates at runtime via active defense-impairment behavior; outside tachi's design-time threat-modeling scope."` and `"Tactic TA0043 operates at runtime via active pre-compromise reconnaissance; outside tachi's design-time threat-modeling scope."`). The 2 derived rationales account for 88 of the 378 OOS records (23.3%); the 7 verbatim rationales account for 289 (76.5%); the 1 T042 per-item override (T1078.004) accounts for 1 (0.3%). Final ATT&CK split: 323 in-scope (46.1%) + 378 OOS (53.9%).

**Consequence**: ATT&CK coverage_percentage on all 8 baselines reads 0.00% post-T053-T055 (numerator zero — none of the source_attribution maps populated by T053-T055 cite ATT&CK as `primary`; CWE references are `related` only and do not increment the covered_count per the aggregator's `relationship == primary` predicate). The denominator is 323 (post-tactical-grouping filter). This is acceptable per SC-007 — the criterion requires "non-zero on at least 1 framework family served"; OWASP satisfies this on every baseline (13.33% – 26.67% spread). Cross-framework primary attribution (citing ATT&CK / ATLAS / NIST AI RMF / CWE as `primary` rather than OWASP-only) is deferred to D-12 as a deliberate Tier-2 closure scope; future hypothetical F-9+ work would extend the populator-wiring template to emit cross-framework primaries.

**Forward-scope follow-on (data-model.md §5 update)**: A non-breaking spec additive recommended at Wave 6 polish: extend `data-model.md` §5 with +2 entries enumerating TA0112 Defense Impairment + TA0043 Reconnaissance using the derived rationale strings. If absorbed at the F-241 plan-day cleanup commit or as a follow-on `docs(241)` commit before delivery, the spec authority and the catalog implementation align cleanly. If not absorbed, this D-5 narrative serves as the build-time discovery audit and the spec authority gap is a known-deferred item.

### D-6: ATLAS + OWASP Audit-Only Scope

**Decision**: Expand ATLAS 12 → 30 records with per-item Out-of-Scope on runtime/IR-only techniques (T039 expansion concluded all 30 IN-SCOPE because each ATLAS entry has at least one design-time analog — no per-item OOS applied at the ATLAS layer). **Stream**: Stream 3

**Context**: MITRE ATLAS (Adversarial Threat Landscape for Artificial-Intelligence Systems) is a sister catalog to ATT&CK Enterprise focused on AI/ML adversarial techniques. ATLAS at PRD time carried 12 records in `schemas/taxonomy/mitre-atlas.yaml`; T038/T039 expansion to 30 records used the `atlas-data` source (https://github.com/mitre-atlas/atlas-data) per T038's source-discipline contract. OWASP at PRD time was already populated at 60 records (10 entries per framework × 6 frameworks: A0X-2021 Web Top 10, API0X-2023 API Top 10, ASI0X-2026 Agentic Security Top 10, LLM0X:2025 LLM Top 10, MX-2024 Mobile Top 10, ML0X:2023 ML Top 10) per the F-A1 / F-241 PRD §6 framework enumeration; T036/T037 audit confirmed all 60 in-scope and gap-free at the citation-completeness layer (with two pre-existing audit failures carried forward from F-A1 work — A05 host-citation + API7 SSRF pattern-category — which are tracked as follow-on Issues per D-4 Consequence).

Denominator is 30 (no per-item OOS filter applied). The asymmetry to ATT&CK Enterprise (where 7 tactics reduce 701 → 323) is by design — ATLAS is small enough that per-item review is tractable, and the per-item review surfaced no Out-of-Scope items. ### D-13: Auto-Activation Contract for Deferred Parametrization (CHECKPOINT 5 surfacing)

**Decision**: Codify the Mode (a) deferred parametrization pattern from T049 as a **testable invariant pattern** for future BLP features authoring baselines incrementally. Pattern: tests parametrize over `{set_of_active_baselines + set_of_deferred_baselines}`; deferred baselines emit `pytest.skip(...)` markers under controlled conditions (baseline missing, schema mismatch, etc.); regen pipeline removes deferred markers, transitioning skips → passes automatically. Wave 5.2 T057 validation: 30 active + 10 deferred → **48/48 PASS** under controlled regen confirms the contract. Future BLP features should adopt this pattern when rolling out incremental baseline authoring.

**Stream**: Stream 1 / Cross-cutting

**Context**: At Wave 5.1 T049, the tester authored `tests/scripts/test_coverage_percentage_computation.py` with 48 test cases including 40 baseline-framework cross-check pairs (8 baselines × 5 frameworks). At test authoring time, 2 of the 8 baselines (predictive-ml-app + mobile-banking-app) did NOT yet have authored baselines — they were Wave 5.2 T054/T055 deliverables. Two pathways were available:

- **Mode (a) Deferred parametrization**: include all 40 pairs in the parametrization; emit `pytest.skip(...)` on the 10 deferred pairs (predictive-ml-app × 5 frameworks + mobile-banking-app × 5 frameworks) with explicit reason naming T054/T055; document inline at top-of-file AUTHOR'S NOTE that "When Wave 5.2 lands, the skip markers can be lifted with no other source change — the test auto-expands to the documented 40-pair matrix."
- **Mode (b) Active-only parametrization**: include only the 30 active pairs (6 pre-existing baselines × 5 frameworks); add the 10 net-new pairs at Wave 5.2 T057.

Mode (a) was selected at T049 because it makes the test's scope visible at Wave 5.1 (the future maintainer reading T049 can see the full 40-pair matrix and understand the deferred items as a documented forward state). Mode (b) would have hidden the future state inside Wave 5.2 expansion, which would have surprised maintainers reading T049 in isolation.

**Wave 5.2 T057 validation**: At Wave 5.2, the tester lifted the 10 `pytest.skip(...)` markers per the AUTHOR'S NOTE contract. The lift required two simultaneous changes: (1) removing `deferral_reason` strings from the `BASELINES` tuple (3-tuple to 4-tuple shape extension carrying the optional `compensating_controls_md_relative_path` field); (2) adding Tier-1 vs Tier-3 path selection inside the test method to mirror the runtime aggregator's tier-selection at `extract-report-data.py:2079-2110` (Tier-1 if cc.md present → `parse_compensating_controls_md` + merge from Section 9, NOT Tier-3 → `parse_threats_findings` only). The Tier-1 mirroring discovery — that the Wave 5.1 Mode (a) implementation had implicitly assumed Tier-3 parsing for all 8 baselines, when in fact 3 baselines (maestro-reference, predictive-ml-app, mobile-banking-app) are Tier-1 — was a scope refinement, not a scope violation: the tester correctly identified the gap and corrected via the proper architectural path (mirror runtime tier-selection) rather than a band-aid fix.

The auto-activation contract held: 30 active + 10 deferred → **48/48 PASS** (40 active pairs + 8 edge-case tests) under Wave 5.2 controlled regen, 0 NEW pytest regressions vs Wave 5.1 baseline. This validates the Mode (a) pattern as a stable testable invariant for future BLP features authoring baselines incrementally.

**Consequence**: Future BLP features rolling out incremental baseline authoring should adopt this pattern: parametrize tests over the full target baseline set at authoring time; emit `pytest.skip(...)` on deferred baselines with explicit reason naming the future task ID; document inline AUTHOR'S NOTE the auto-activation pathway. KB-037-anchored: T046 KB-037 invariant guard (`tests/scripts/test_pyyaml_deferred_import.py`) AST walks all `scripts/*.py` to enforce the stdlib-only module-load invariant; the same auto-activation discipline applied to deferred parametrization gives future maintainers a clear forward-state visibility without requiring out-of-band coordination. The contract refinement at T057 (Tier-1 mirroring) is a doctrine-level result: future tests verifying parser-output equality MUST mirror the runtime tier-selection rule, not assume Tier-3 by default. Both the Mode (a) deferral pattern and the runtime-mirroring doctrine are candidate refinements to the BLP-01 §8 Quality Bar; their codification at this D-13 narrative makes them visible to future BLP maintainers.
